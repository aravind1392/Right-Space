5 Modular Kitchen Layouts That Actually Work

◷ 5 min read✎ Right Space Design Team◆ June 2026
On this page

A beautiful kitchen that is annoying to cook in is a failed kitchen. Before finishes and colours, the single most important decision is the layout — how the space is organised around the way you actually cook. Get it right and everything flows; get it wrong and you will feel it three times a day.

Here are the five modular kitchen layouts we design most often for homes across Coimbatore, and how to choose between them.

Start with the work triangle

Every good kitchen layout respects the work triangle — the path between your sink, hob and refrigerator. Keep those three within comfortable reach of each other (roughly 4–8 metres total) and you avoid the constant back-and-forth that makes cooking tiring. Layout choices below are really different ways of arranging that triangle around your room.

1. L-shaped

Two connected runs of cabinetry along adjoining walls. It is the most versatile layout, works in open-plan homes, and leaves room for a small dining table. Ideal for small-to-medium kitchens and the layout we recommend most often for apartments.

2. U-shaped

Three walls of storage and counter space. Maximum worktop and cabinet capacity, and a very efficient triangle. Best for medium-to-large kitchens where at least three walls are available.

3. Parallel / galley

Two facing runs with a walkway between. Extremely efficient for serious cooking and great for narrow spaces. Keep the gap between counters at around 1.1–1.2 m so two people can pass comfortably.

4. Island

An L or U layout plus a freestanding island. The island adds prep space, storage and casual seating, and anchors an open-plan kitchen-diner. It needs floor area — allow at least a metre of clearance all around — so it suits larger villas and open homes.

5. Straight-line

A single run along one wall. Perfect for compact apartments and studio layouts, and increasingly popular in open-plan homes where the kitchen is part of the living space. Pair it with tall units to claw back storage.

Key takeaways

  • Choose the layout before the finishes — flow beats looks.
  • L-shaped is the safest all-rounder; U-shaped maximises storage.
  • Islands need space; straight-line and galley suit compact homes.
  • Protect the sink–hob–fridge work triangle in every layout.
